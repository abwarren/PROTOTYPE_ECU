# The Problem

## The Performance ECU Market is Stuck

The market for standalone engine control units has three structural problems that create an opportunity.

### 1. Legacy Software

The dominant ECU tuning software hasn't evolved in a decade. The user interfaces look and feel like Windows 95 applications. Key features include:

- Grey, cluttered interfaces
- Outdated gauge rendering
- No dark mode
- No cloud connectivity
- No mobile companion apps
- No AI-assisted tuning
- Limited data visualization

Workshops and tuners spend 95% of their working hours in these applications. Yet customers accept the experience because there are no better alternatives.

### 2. High Cost Barrier

Professional ECUs cost $3,000-8,000+ per unit. For a workshop stocking 5-10 units, that's $15,000-80,000 in inventory alone. For enthusiasts building a single project car, it's a significant portion of their budget.

This pricing is not driven by hardware cost — it's driven by market inertia and lack of competition at the quality level.

### 3. Fragmented Ecosystem

Most ECU products exist in isolation:

- Proprietary firmware that doesn't talk to other systems
- No cloud backup of calibrations
- No remote diagnostics
- No OTA updates
- No fleet management
- No integration with modern workshop tools

Workshops manage multiple ECUs across multiple brands with no unified platform.

## The Market Is Ready for Change

- More performance vehicles on the road than ever
- A new generation of tuners who expect modern software experiences
- Workshops looking for competitive advantage through better tools
- Open-source ECU firmware has proven the technology is accessible
- Cloud infrastructure is cheap and reliable enough for automotive use

## Why Now?

The convergence of:
1. Mature open-source ECU firmware (rusEFI has 10+ years of development)
2. Modern desktop frameworks (Electron, React) that make premium UIs accessible
3. Cheap cloud infrastructure (AWS IoT, managed MQTT, TimescaleDB)
4. A generation of tuners who expect mobile apps and cloud connectivity
5. Competitors who have not innovated in years

The window is open for a platform that delivers modern software with proven firmware at a fraction of the incumbent cost.
